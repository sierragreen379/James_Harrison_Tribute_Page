James Harrison

Man with the Golden Arm

Who is he?

James Harrison is a superhero. It's in his blood.

Literally.

Harrison's blood plasma contains a rare antibody called Anti-D, which is essentially an antidote to hemolytic disease of the newborn (HDN) in unborn children. From this antibody, scientists made a vaccine that has saved over 2.5 million babies' lives.

Timeline of Harrison's life

  • 1936: Born in Austrailia.
  • 1951: Undergoes major chest surgery that requires a lot of blood. Because of this, he vowed to start donating blood himself once he turned 18 (the then-required age).
  • 1954: Starts donating blood. It only takes a few donations before scientists realize his blood had unusually strong and persistent antibodies to the D Rh group antigen. They then created a vaccine to be used on pregnant mothers to protect their babies from HDN.
  • 1954-2018: Donates blood plasma every three weeks on average.
  • 1999: Awarded the Medal of the Order of Austrailia.
  • 2011: Reaches his 1,000th donation.
  • 2018: Makes his 1,173rd donation, which was also his last, as Australian policy does not allow those past 81 years of age to donate.
